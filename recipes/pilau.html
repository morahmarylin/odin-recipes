Pilau

Here is a recipe for a classic East African beef pilau, a fragrant and flavorful rice dish.

    Ingredients

    • 3 tbsp cooking oil or ghee
    • 2 medium onions, sliced or chopped
    • 1 lb (500g) beef, cut into cubes
    • 1 tbsp garlic-ginger mince (or paste)
    • 1 heaped tbsp whole pilau mix (cumin, cardamom pods, black peppercorns, cinnamon stick, cloves)
    • 2 beef stock cubes (or to taste)
    • Salt to taste
    • 3 medium potatoes, peeled and halved/quartered (optional)
    • 3 cups basmati rice, rinsed and drained
    • 3 & 1/2 cups beef broth (from boiling the beef) or water

      Steps

    1. Prepare the Beef: Boil the cubed beef in about 1 liter of water with some salt until tender. Once cooked, reserve approximately 3.5 cups of the beef broth and set the beef aside.
    2. Sauté Aromatics: In a large pot, heat the oil or ghee over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and sauté until they turn a deep golden brown (this caramelization is key for the pilau color and flavor).
    3. Add Spices and Garlic: Add the garlic-ginger mince and the whole pilau spices mix. Stir constantly and cook for about a minute until fragrant and the aroma fills the kitchen.
    4. Incorporate Beef and Potatoes: Add the pre-boiled beef cubes and optional potatoes to the pot. Mix well and cook for a few minutes to allow the meat to absorb the flavors.
    5. Add Liquid and Rice: Crumble in the stock cubes and mix. Pour in the measured beef broth and bring the mixture to a simmer. Add the rinsed basmati rice, stirring gently to combine.
    6. Cook the Pilau: Bring everything back to a boil, then reduce the heat to the lowest setting. Cover the pot tightly with a lid (using aluminum foil before the lid can help trap the steam effectively).
    7. Steam and Serve: Steam for about 15-20 minutes, or until all the liquid has been absorbed and the rice is cooked through. Turn off the heat and let it sit for another 10 minutes before fluffing with a fork.

    Recipe Tip

    Serve your pilau hot, typically with a side of kachumbari (fresh tomato and onion salad).
