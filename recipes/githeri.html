Recipe: Githeri

Here is a recipe for a Traditional Kenyan Githeri, a deliciously and brilliantly cooked mixture of Maize and Beans.

Githeri is a one pot traditional Kikuyu dish that is made from beans and white maize (corn).

It is a delicious, easy to prepare, protein rich dish which can be enjoyed on its own, with Ugali or Kenyan Chapati.

    Ingredients

    • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
    • 1 medium onion, chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
    • 2 large tomatoes, diced or pureed
    • 1½ tsp smoked paprika
    • ½ tsp curry powder (optional)
    • 2 cups cooked beans (kidney beans or pinto beans work well)
    • 1 cup corn kernels (white corn is traditional, but sweet corn works)
    • ¼ cup vegetable stock or water
    • Salt and black pepper to taste
    • Fresh cilantro (coriander) for garnish (optional)

      Instructions

    1. Sauté Aromatics: Heat the oil in a large pot or pan over medium heat.

      Add the chopped onion and cook until soft and translucent, about 3-5 minutes.

      If using, add the garlic, ginger, and/or optional carrots and cook for another minute until fragrant.

    2. Add Spices and Tomatoes: Stir in the smoked paprika and curry powder and cook for about one minute to release their aroma.

      Add the tomatoes and cook for 3-5 minutes until they break down into a soft, mushy sauce.

    3. Combine Githeri Mix: Add the pre-boiled beans and corn to the pot. Stir well to combine them with the tomato mixture.

    4. Simmer: Pour in the vegetable stock or water and add salt and pepper.

      Cover the pot and reduce the heat to low. Let it simmer for about 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together and the sauce to thicken slightly.

    5. Garnish and Serve: Turn off the heat. Stir in the fresh cilantro just before serving. Serve hot on its own, or with a side of ugali, rice, or chapati.

    Recipe Tip

    Serve your Githeri hot, typically with a slice of ovacado and milk tea.
